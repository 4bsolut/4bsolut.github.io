Zero-downtime deployment checklist

Published April 1, 2024

Zero-downtime releases are possible when you design for rollback and have the right traffic controls.

Automate health checks

Define readiness and liveness probes that reflect real service health, not just process uptime.

Use blue-green or canary strategies

Shift traffic gradually and monitor error budgets. Keep rollback paths fast and documented.

Protect data migrations

Use expand-and-contract changes. Avoid destructive database migrations during the release window.

Practice recovery

Run game days to validate rollback speed and data integrity. Practice is what keeps downtime low.

Zero downtime is a system of discipline, tooling, and rehearsal.
